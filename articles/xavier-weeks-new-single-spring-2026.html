Xavier Weeks Announces New Single for Spring 2026

News, Releases & Insights

Release

Xavier Weeks is a genre-defying artist who's built a name across the independent music world. With over 250 million streams, he first drew attention with his EP Night Drive, but it was his follow-up work that cemented his position as one of the most compelling voices in contemporary music.

Apple Music wrote that "his ability to marry analog warmth with digital production is virtually unmatched among his generation." His live shows, equal parts folk intimacy and cinematic grandeur, have been described as transformative experiences that transcend the typical concert format.

Now, in partnership with Akasha Music, Xavier is entering a new chapter. The forthcoming single represents a deliberate evolution — drawing from time spent between studios in Reykjavik and Los Angeles — working across textural electronic landscapes and raw vocal performances, pushing further into territory that is distinctly, unmistakably his own. It arrives at a moment of intense creative momentum and will debut exclusively via streaming platforms before a wider release.

"Music isn't just sound. It's a world you build around yourself."

The upcoming single represents what the artist describes as "the closest thing to a diary entry I've ever released." Drawing from personal experiences and recorded across sessions that spanned continents — from late-night studio sessions in Reykjavik to sun-drenched mornings in Los Angeles — the track blends analog textures with contemporary electronic production in a way that feels both intimate and expansive. Fans who have followed Xavier's evolution from his early folk-influenced work to the critically acclaimed Night Drive EP will find familiar emotional depth wrapped in an entirely new sonic architecture.

Background

Xavier Weeks is a genre-fluid artist with a unique sense for bridging the personal and the universal. With over 250 million total streams to his name, he first caught the ear of critics with his 2024 EP Night Drive, a collection that Music Ally called "quietly magnetic." Since then, he's collaborated with producers across three continents and built a live show that has become a destination in its own right.

Now, in partnership with Akasha Music, Xavier is entering a new era. His upcoming single represents a deliberate evolution — drawing from months spent between Reykjavik and Los Angeles. The studio sessions were described by collaborators as some of the most focused and emotionally present work Xavier has ever done, pushing further into electronic territory while retaining the raw vocal intimacy that first drew listeners in.

"Music isn't just sound. It's a world you build around yourself."

The single arrives alongside a broader creative rollout that includes visual collaborations, an expanded touring schedule, and a new approach to fan engagement from the artist. With its release tied to the SXSW calendar, the timing suggests ambitions that extend well beyond streaming numbers — this is an artist using a single release as a launchpad for an entirely new creative chapter.
